<p><p>The Body Exfoliator Exfoliating Agent market comprises two main types: physical and chemical exfoliating agents. Physical exfoliators involve granular substances that manually slough off dead skin cells through scrubbing. Common examples include sugar, salt, or ground kernels. In contrast, chemical exfoliators utilize acids or enzymes to dissolve dead skin cells, promoting cell turnover without scrubbing. These agents often include alpha-hydroxy acids (AHAs) and beta-hydroxy acids (BHAs), which provide a more gentle and effective exfoliation suitable for various skin types.</p></p>
