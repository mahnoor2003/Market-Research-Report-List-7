<p><p>Recycled fiber fabrics for apparel are derived from two primary systems: cellulose and protein. The cellulose system includes fibers obtained from recycled plant materials, such as cotton or wood pulp, known for their breathability and sustainable properties. The protein system focuses on fibers made from recycled animal sources like wool or silk, appreciated for their insulation and durability. Both systems contribute to a more sustainable fashion industry by reducing waste and minimizing environmental impact while offering unique textures and qualities in clothing.</p></p>
