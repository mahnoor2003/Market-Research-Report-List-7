<p><p>The Puer tea market is divided into two main types: Raw Puer tea and Cooked Puer tea. Raw Puer tea, also known as Sheng, is naturally fermented and has a vibrant, complex flavor profile that matures over time. It is often prized for its health benefits and aging potential. In contrast, Cooked Puer tea, or Shu, undergoes a rapid fermentation process, resulting in a smoother, earthy taste that appeals to consumers seeking an immediate, rich flavor. Both types cater to diverse palates and preferences.</p></p>
