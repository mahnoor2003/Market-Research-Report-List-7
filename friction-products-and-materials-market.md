<p><p>The friction products and materials market is divided into two main types: dry friction materials and wet friction materials. Dry friction materials, commonly used in automotive brake pads and industrial clutches, rely on mechanical interlocking and surface roughness to provide friction without lubrication. In contrast, wet friction materials operate in lubricated environments, such as automotive transmissions, where they utilize fluid lubricants to enhance performance and reduce wear. Both types are crucial for ensuring efficient transmission of force in various applications.</p></p>
