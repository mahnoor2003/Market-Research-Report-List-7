<p><p>The XBC battery market encompasses various types of silicon wafers, primarily P-Type and N-Type. P-Type silicon wafers are derived from boron-doped silicon, featuring holes as charge carriers, making them widely used in traditional solar cells. In contrast, N-Type silicon wafers are phosphorus-doped, offering improved efficiency and better performance in high-temperature environments. The distinction between these wafer types influences energy conversion efficiency, durability, and overall battery performance, driving advancements in energy storage technologies and applications in renewable energy systems.</p></p>
