<p><ul><li>Antepartum Fetal Monitoring</li><li>Intrapartum Fetal Monitoring</li></ul></p>
<p><p>Fetal monitoring encompasses two primary types: antepartum and intrapartum. Antepartum fetal monitoring occurs during pregnancy before labor, focusing on assessing the fetus's well-being and identifying potential complications through techniques like ultrasound and non-stress tests. In contrast, intrapartum fetal monitoring takes place during labor, monitoring the fetus's heart rate and response to contractions to ensure safe delivery. Both types are crucial for optimizing maternal and fetal health outcomes, guiding clinical decisions throughout the pregnancy and childbirth process.</p></p>
